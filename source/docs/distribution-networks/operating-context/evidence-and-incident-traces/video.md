# Video

Video adds the layer the other sources leave out: the embodied interaction, which screen, which soft-key, which physical
handle, in what order, and which action the system refuses.

Relay testing is the richest and most public genre: A 
[step-by-step tutorial on GOOSE between DIGSI 4 and DIGSI 5](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6sPQs9fjOi0) works
through configuring and testing the messaging across SIPROTEC 4 and SIPROTEC 5,
a [Siemens wiring-test walkthrough](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X-PyzUIMt08) covers SIPROTEC 5 using SIPROTEC Tools,
a [secondary-injection test](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3-kLCfbeA-4) runs against the Omicron CMC 356,
a [DIGSI 5 and SIPROTEC DigitalTwin](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cRunTbOux64) session sets up a test mode on the
7SX85, and [OMICRON's own series](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLD2310E4449DD3D32) covers testing of protection
and measurement equipment.

Retrieving stored evidence through the engineering tool is the subject made literal in a walkthrough
of [downloading the record and fault logs from a SIPROTEC 5 relay using DIGSI 5](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gU3znCgKnzw),
the engineering workstation reaching into the relay to pull the event and disturbance records the device retains.

Commissioning overlaps that cluster, since the wiring-test and DigitalTwin sessions are commissioning-phase activities.
A commissioning video verifies that the CILO interlock releases across input combinations and traces a signal through
the substation automation system to the control centre. The interlock that refuses to open a disconnector under load is
the same CILO logical node encoded in open-source substation models.

A control-room 
[virtual tour of SurvalentONE SmartVU](https://www.survalent.com/cat-video/virtual-part-tour-2-overview-of-survalentone-adms-applications/)
is described by the vendor as a guided walkthrough of a real-world setup, operators and dispatchers monitoring outages,
feeder health, substations and KPIs across integrated SCADA, OMS and DMS. Survalent is not Stedin's platform, which runs
e-terra under GE.

Stedin's own [recruitment and open-day material](https://werkenbij.stedin.net/) shows staff at real high-voltage
stations. The generic workflow is on film in abundance; the specific Stedin switching sequence and the e-terra screens
stay inside the control room.
