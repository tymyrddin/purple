# Elephants all the way down

Roleplay does not have to stop at the tabletop. Blue's
[Campaigns, manoeuvres and scenarios](https://blue.tymyrddin.dev/docs/scenarios/) runs it at the scale of a
whole geopolitical ecosystem: a city and its Establishment, its most-watched ally Fungolia, the red-team
consultancy that probes them, the regional registry whose routes the entire frontier hears, and the ministry
that investigates its own sisters. Each is a standing premise, and inside each sit smaller roleplays: a single
incident, a defender watching their own border, a breach reconstructed one step at a time.

It is wheels within wheels. A nation-state manoeuvre contains an institutional exercise contains a single
incident-response drill, and the same event reads differently at each ring: an outright BGP hijack at the top,
a defender proving that every route is valid and something is still wrong at the bottom. The premise recurses,
and a group can enter at whichever ring it can hold.

The defender's side of these lives in blue. The attacker's side runs from the other seat in red's earthworks,
where [MycoSec](https://red.tymyrddin.dev/docs/earthworks/mycosec/), the Fungolian red-team consultancy, keeps
deliberately exposed labs that make the ground for the exercises. Purple's interest is the loop between the
two: a roleplay earns its place when both seats are played by people who will have to live with what the
exercise surfaces, rather than narrated from one side.
