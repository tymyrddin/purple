# Acting on findings

An engagement can find exactly what it set out to find, produce an accurate report, and change nothing.
When that happens the reason is rarely that the finding was wrong. It is that the conditions for the
finding to travel were absent.

Three layers decide whether a finding moves, following the
[three-layer frame](../foundations/problem-solving/in-security.md). The rational layer, whether the finding is
accurate and specific, is the easy part and the one exercises usually get right. The emotional and political
layers are where findings stall.

- The emotional layer is whether the room was [safe enough](../engagement/safety.md) for the real finding to be named. A finding
  that asks someone to admit a critical service could be down for a week surfaces accurately only when the
  person who would own that admission is present and willing to make it. Where naming it carries a cost,
  the sanitised version gets recorded instead, and the sanitised version cannot be acted on.
- The political layer is whether anyone present can turn the finding into a decision. Someone, by name,
  needs the authority and the will to convert it into a changed control, a moved budget or a different
  process. Where security sits below the line at which budgets are set, findings become a list that is
  acknowledged and left unresourced.
- The finding needs an owner from the moment it is written. A finding specific enough to be given an owner
  is usually specific enough to act on, and a finding too vague to own is too vague to be a finding. A
  priority finding whose owner is "nobody present" is the signal to bring the right person in or to
  escalate, before the exercise closes rather than after.

The practical test is the one [threat modelling](../threat-modelling/bringing-it-together.md) applies
before a session ends: for each priority finding, who in the room has the authority and the capacity to
act on it. A programme that can answer that produces change; one that cannot produces a document. The same
conditions run through [engagement objectives](../engagement/objectives.md), the
[readiness](../purple/whether-you-are-ready.md) a purple loop needs before it starts, and the reporting
[handover](../audits/supportive/findings-reporting.md) that carries a finding to whoever can act on it.
